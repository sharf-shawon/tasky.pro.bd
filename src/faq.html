Frequently Asked Questions

Where is my data stored?

Your data never leaves your machine. Tasky is a "local-first" application, meaning all your lists and tasks are stored directly in your browser's local storage. We do not have a server, and we cannot see your tasks.

Is my data secure?

Yes. All data stored in your browser is encrypted using AES-GCM (256-bit) encryption. We use the Web Crypto API to derive a unique device key that stays on your computer. Even if someone gained access to your browser's files, your tasks remain unreadable without the key.

How does sharing work?

When you share a list, Tasky generates a unique link containing the encrypted data of that list. You can optionally protect this link with a password. If you use a password, the data is encrypted again using that password (PBKDF2) before being added to the URL. The recipient needs the link (and the password, if set) to decrypt and import the list.

What are the core features?
  • Create multiple task lists.
  • Drag and drop items within or between lists.
  • Client-side encryption for everything.
  • Secure list sharing via URL or QR Code.
  • Multiple themes (Dark, Light, Cyberpunk, etc.).
  • Personalized colors for every list.
  • Full data backup (JSON or Encrypted).
Is Tasky free?

Yes. Tasky is an open-source tool and is completely free to use. You can even self-host it yourself using our Docker setup.
